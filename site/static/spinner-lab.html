Loading, with a point of view.

Ten ways to turn the Aperture ring into a loading signal—from quiet inline feedback to a branded full-surface moment.

Start with Iris sequence

It remains legible at 16 px, feels native to the mark, and avoids the generic “spinning wheel” look.

Recommended

01 · Default

Iris sequence

Light walks around the six blades. Distinctive, calm, and still clearly the LeapView mark.

02 · Familiar

Ring trace

A moving perimeter keeps the aperture still. Familiar spinner behavior with a branded center.

03 · Energetic

Orbit

The full aperture turns beneath a small tracking point. Best for short, energetic transitions.

04 · Steady

Continuous spin

A constant linear rotation with no easing or pause. Simple, predictable, and visibly different from Orbit.

05 · Quiet

Breathing mark

A soft scale and opacity rhythm for background refreshes where rotation would feel too loud.

06 · Surface

Counter halo

The outer halo rotates while the mark holds position. Strong for cards, charts, and page-level loading.

07 · Determinate

Progress aperture

Use the ring as real progress—not decorative motion—when the backend can report a percentage.

Keep it thin

Use the logo’s 1.8 stroke throughout. A heavier loader feels like a different identity.

Delay before showing

Wait roughly 200–300 ms before revealing a spinner so fast actions do not flicker.

Always name the wait

The visual is decorative. Pair it with a status label for assistive technology and longer operations.
